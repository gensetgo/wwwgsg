When sudden load is put on an single cylinder engine, it sufferes a temporary "setback" - engine slows down for a fraction of second before coming back to normal state. If you have too high a sudden load, engine might be knocked down completely. Comparatively, if this load is distributed on, say two cylinders, both would be able to "react" to this setback jointly in a much better way. The more the cylinder the better the response.
Result
  • Leypower engine is able to respond to start-up jerks much gracefully

The startup jerk, which is associated with temporary slowdown of engine, results in momentary decrease in voltage and frequency. Electronic components are very sensitive to frequency. These initial load jerks, even though it lasts only for fraction of second, are lethal for electronic equipments - it literally kills them. You mght have observed that CFL develops black patch at the end if there are voltage fluctuation.

Never Shutdown Engine
How long can you run your car nonstop? 4 hours? 10 hours? 24 hours? Most engines need to be shut down after a limited perid of operations. However, there are scenarios when a engine just cannot be allowed to go down – EVER !! eg. in power house , oil well etc.
Such engines are said to be of Continous Rating or Prime Rating in contrast to the other types that are called Stand-by Rating.
Stand-by rated engines can run for limited hours only after which it should be brought down for cooling, maintenance etc.
Continuous rating engines, on the other hand, once started need not be stopped again. Engines used in Ashok Leyland, Leypower DG Set are all continuous rated (except 500 kVA). Such engines need special manufacturing and design skills and not many companies in the world have the technolgy to make Continous Rating engines. Obviously, such engines are much more expensive.
